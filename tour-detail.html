We're excited to welcome you to Hilltop Retreat, a brand new cabin with the best views in Broken Bow! We personally manage this property and are dedicated to making your stay memorable and special.

Our cabin features modern design elements, Smart TVs in every room, a fully-equipped kitchen with complimentary wine, coffee, and tea, luxurious bathroom amenities, and an outdoor TV nearside a shuffleboard, board game table, and arcade. Firewood is also provided for the fire pit and grill.

The living area has an open floor plan across two stories, with a comfortable leather sofa, Smart TVs with Dish satellite cable, and a cozy gas fireplace.

The kitchen is fully equipped and features quartz countertops and stainless steel appliances. Prepare delicious meals and enjoy them at the dining table that seats eight or at the kitchen bar with seating for four.

The cabin sleeps up to 14, with three king suites, two full-size futons, and a triple bunk. Each bedroom has a balcony door that opens up to the back deck with a stunning view of the mountains. All three bedrooms also have en suite bathrooms with walk-in showers (one has a soaking tub).

Our amazing outdoor space boasts two decks for your enjoyment, along with a fire pit area. We welcome pets on a case-by-case basis, with a pet fee of $50/night.

Please note, our cabin is equipped with outdoor security cameras for your safety. There are no cameras inside the house.

  1. Day 1 Arrival

    Arrive in Broken Bow and check into your cabin. Many guests that plan to cook often pick up groceries prior to check-in. The kitchen comes fully-stocked with all cooking essentials, so it's a great way to get settled in. In the evening, enjoy a campfire and s'mores at your private fire pit.

  2. Day 2 Hiking & Scenery

    Spend the afternoon hiking on one of the many trails in Beavers Bend State Park, such as the Lookout Mountain Trail or the Cedar Bluff Nature Trail. This is a great way to explore the beauty of Oklahoma and get some exercise.

  3. Day 3 Lake Day

    In the morning, go kayaking or river floating on the Beavers Bend Reservoir. This is a great way to cool off on a hot day and enjoy the scenery. In the afternoon, take a Tiki Boat Tour on the lake. These tours are a fun and relaxing way to see the area and learn about the history of the lake.

  4. Day 4 Wine & Dine

    In the morning, go wine tasting at Fish Tales or Knotted Rope Winery. These wineries offer a variety of wines to sample, and you can also enjoy a delicious lunch on the patio. In the afternoon, visit the Hochatown Distillery for a tour and tasting. This distillery produces award-winning whiskey, vodka, and gin.

  5. Day 5 Hangover Recovery

    In the morning, visit The Maze. This is a fun and challenging maze that is perfect for a family outing. In the afternoon, go horseback riding with Riverman Trail Rides. It is the perfect place for you to recharge and admire nature.

  6. Day 6 Fishing & Lake Day

    In the morning, go fishing at the Beavers Bend Reservoir. This is a great place to catch bass, catfish, and crappie. In the afternoon, go for a swim at one of the many swimming holes in the area. You also have your own private hot tub overlooking the Broken Bow hills if you prefer.

  7. Day 7 Shopping & Souvenirs

    In the morning, go shopping at the Broken Bow Mercantile. This store has a variety of unique items, including souvenirs, clothing, and gifts. In the afternoon, have dinner at one of the many restaurants in Broken Bow.

Broken Bow offers a wide variety of outdoor and indoor activities to keep you entertained throughout your stay:

  • Outdoor Recreation: The cabin is located near Beavers Bend State Park and Hochatown State Park, both of which offer a wealth of outdoor activities. You can enjoy hiking on numerous trails with varying difficulty levels, fishing in the clear mountain streams, or boating on Broken Bow Lake. Kayaking, canoeing, and paddle boarding are also popular activities on the lake.
  • Wildlife Viewing: The parks are a nature lover's paradise. They are home to many different species of birds, deer, and other wildlife. Be sure to bring your binoculars!
  • Wineries and Breweries: Hochatown, near Broken Bow, has several local wineries and breweries. You can enjoy a tasting and even take a tour to learn about the production process.
  • Dining and Shopping: Broken Bow boasts a variety of restaurants offering local and international cuisine. The town also has several unique boutiques and antique stores, perfect for those looking for a souvenir or gift.
  • Art and Culture: Visit the Forest Heritage Center to learn about the local history and culture. The Museum of the Red River also offers fascinating exhibits about the region.
  • Adventure Activities: For the thrill-seekers, there are opportunities for horseback riding, zip-lining, and even ATV riding in the area.

Remember, depending on the season, some activities may not be available. It's always best to check the local tourism websites for the most current information.
